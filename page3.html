Micro - Satellite 

Project:      Design a micro-satellite and a space mission that will rendezvous with a 3U cubesat space debris and capture it. The debris capturing system will be based on a fisherman's net and be composed of carbon fiber, which will be launched from the host satellite. Once the net is launched it will collapse upon itself, engulfing the debris.

Ghantt Chart

ORBITAL

To accomplish the desired goal of an orbital rendezvous and proximity operations in LEO, a simulation program called STK made by the AGI company was used. The target space debris is a Virginia CubeSat Constellation, an Old Dominion University 3U CubeSat

h

Goal

Host Sat rendezvous with VCC and stay within a 100 meter range for at least 30 minutes.

2

Burns 

The first burn will last 994.75 seconds, causing the orbit to change into an elliptical orbit around the earth.
The second burn will last 1290.79 seconds and the Host Sat will circumnavigate the debris, keeping within range.   

3

Results 

The total time from the first burn to end of the final burn is 11 hours 32 minutes and 32 seconds and the total fuel required is 388.434 kg.
The simulation demonstrates the Host Sat staying within 10 meters for 13 hours.
Once the 5m by 5m drag sail is deployed the Host Sat along with the debris will re-enter the atmosphere and burn after 607.7 days.

Launcher

8 Weights, weighing 2.5 kg each are attached and angled at 45 degrees.  
Each weight has a thin foam sponge sleeve.  
Using pressurized gas the net will be shot out at roughly 1 m/s and the weights at 1.4 m/s relative to the host sat. The estimated constant pressures required are relatively low at about 0.5 kPa for the net and 27 kPa for each of the weights. The pressures are expected to increase as testing on how the net expands effects the resulting speed. 

Host Sat

The purpose of the Host Satellite is to contain the capturing mechanism. The design is based on cube satellites which are a common way of creating satellites. The Host Satellite is equipped with a Zigbee communication device, Mast cameras, fuel and pressure tank storages, drag sail, and net capturing system. The motivation of this design was to create a simplistic Host Satellite that could be manufactured in abundance, to capture many debris at a relatively low cost. Furthermore, with the incorporation of a drag sail, the Host Satellite is designed to never reach ultimate failure which is the creation of more debris in Earth's orbit.

Tether 
The tether is 100 m long to account for possible error in the orbital simulations
CFCC was again utilized to facilitate weight requirements
A 0.0343 m diameter gives the tether a breaking load of 1342 kN
The total weight of the tether is 109.5 kg 

Net 

The design for the net was inspired after a fisherman’s cast net. The intent of the net design was to create a net that was large to increase the probability of debris capture, while also keeping the weight down as much as possible. Reducing the weight of the net was a critical design task as a lighter net meant that the amount of fuel required would decrease and the amount of pressurized gas needed to launch the net would also decrease.

Size/ Weight

The net was designed to have a 20 meter diameter with a 100 millimeter gap between each strand encompassing the area. The lowest possible weight for the net is 106.24 kg. 

Calculaltions 

Our target applies a force of 1.3597 N and creats a shear stress of 4251.6 N/m2. The carbon fiber net  allowable Shear Stress of 1 GPa

Results 

From the calculations that were performed on the carbon fiber net it will withstand the forces and the stresses that will be applied during impact from the debris.
